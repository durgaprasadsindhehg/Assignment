David Warner retires from ODI cricket before Test farewell, marks end of an era in Australian cricket

David Warner retires from ODI cricket before Test farewell, marks end of an era in Australian cricket. During a press conference held at the Sydney Cricket Ground, Warner elucidated his decision, stating, "I've got to give back to the family and also on the back of that I'm definitely retiring from one-day cricket as well.""That was something that I had said through the (2023) World Cup, get through that, and winning it in India, I think that's a massive achievement.
Impressive ODI record and future endeavors Exiting the ODI arena after featuring in 161 matches, Warner leaves behind a formidable record of 6,932 runs at an average of 45.30, featuring 22 centuries. Only Ricky Ponting, among Australians, has surpassed Warner's remarkable tally of one-day tons.
